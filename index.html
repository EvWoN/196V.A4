Crypto Market Hype: The Rise and Fall

David Henning | Jason Chernioglo | Cameron Li

0.0 - Some Background

0.1 Not too long-ago cryptocurrencies were a hot topic on almost every media outlet in 2017 and 2018 as the value of them kept growing. It seemed that everyone suddenly had some cryptocurrency.

0.2 Cryptocurrencies even had a major impact on other parts of the technology sector such as Graphics Processing Units (GPUs) being sold out or sold at a premium globally. GPUs are predominantly used to mine these cryptocurrencies.

0.3 What are they? Cryptocurrencies are digital currencies that are secured by cryptography. Cryptocurrencies are often decentralized and instead rely on a publicly shared ledger called a blockchain to keep track of and verify transactions

1.0 - What we want to know

1.1 The questions we want to explore and answer are: Is cryptocurrency dead? How has the sudden spotlight impacted cryptocurrencies? And, should anyone even care anymore?

5.0 - Actually, a Comeback on the rise

5.1 While market pricing is a common and often good indicator for the health of a currency, as we have seen there are flaws in this metric since non-stable variable can cause severe up and down swings not indicative of the actual performance of the cryptocurrency.

5.2 There is a far more reliable indicator for growth, and it paints an entirely different picture of the current state of cryptocurrencies. This metric is the volume of cryptocurrency that is being traded.

5.3 The trade volume metric shows us that the use of cryptocurrencies is in fact higher than ever, even compare to December of 2017 when a bitcoin was valued at $20,000.

Volume vs Time of a selected token

6.0 - Conclusion: What does this mean

6.1 While the hype in 2017 saw huge influx in cryptocurrency projects most of them were scams riding off the media attention that artificially inflated the value of nearly every cryptocurrencies that was announced.

6.2 Cryptocurrencies and Blockchains are in fact as cool as they were made out to be and in 2017 and 2018 big enterprises realized that they needed blockchains.

6.3 Now Google, IBM, Microsoft, Amazon, SWIFT, Oracle and other are all working heavily to develop blockchain technology.

6.4 It is expected that in 2021 and 2022 blockchain technology will see massive adoption as services by these enterprises are pushed to the public.

6.5 The market cap bubble was just a mainstream, artificial phenomenon which is overshadowing real and tangible growth occurring right now.
